# AI Tools Staff Training Outline

A modular training plan for rolling out AI tools to non-technical teams. Pick the modules relevant to your rollout phase. Each module is designed as a 30-45 minute session.

---

## Training Philosophy

Most AI training fails because it starts with the technology. Start with the problems your staff already has, then show how AI helps solve them. People adopt tools that make their day easier, not tools that sound impressive.

---

## Module 1: AI Fundamentals (Required for All Staff)

**Goal:** Everyone understands what AI can and can't do, and why we have a policy.

**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Topics:**
- What AI actually is (and isn't), in plain terms
- How large language models work at a high level (input goes in, prediction comes out, it's not "thinking")
- What AI is good at: drafting, summarizing, brainstorming, translating, pattern recognition
- What AI is bad at: facts, math, nuance, context it wasn't given, anything requiring real judgment
- Why we have an acceptable use policy (not to slow you down, but to protect patients/students/the organization)
- Where to find the policy and who to ask with questions

**Activity:** Show 3 examples of AI getting something wrong. Have staff identify what went wrong and why.

**Takeaway:** AI is a powerful tool, but you are always responsible for the final output.

---

## Module 2: Data Handling & Classification (Required for All Staff)

**Goal:** Staff can correctly classify data before using it with AI.

**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Topics:**
- The four classification levels (Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted)
- Walk through 10 real scenarios and classify them together
- What PHI is and why it can never go into unapproved tools
- What to do if you accidentally send sensitive data to an AI tool
- The difference between enterprise AI tools and free consumer accounts

**Activity:** Quiz with 10 scenarios. Staff must classify the data and decide if AI can be used.

**Takeaway:** When in doubt, ask IT before pasting. It takes 30 seconds and can prevent a major problem.

---

## Module 3: Using [Specific Tool] (Per Tool Rollout)

**Goal:** Staff can effectively use the approved AI tool for their specific workflows.

**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Topics:**
- How to access the tool (login, SSO, where to find it)
- Basic prompting: how to ask AI for what you actually want
- Common use cases for your department (customize per audience)
- What not to do (with examples from the acceptable use policy)
- How to evaluate AI output before using it
- How to report problems or provide feedback

**Activity:** Hands-on exercise where staff complete a real work task using the tool with guidance.

**Takeaway:** Provide a one-page quick reference card staff can keep at their desk.

---

## Module 4: Prompting for Better Results (Optional, Recommended for Power Users)

**Goal:** Staff who use AI regularly can get significantly better output.

**Duration:** 45 minutes

**Topics:**
- Why the way you ask matters (garbage in, garbage out)
- Be specific: "Summarize this in 3 bullet points for a non-technical audience" vs. "Summarize this"
- Give context: tell the AI who you are, what you need, and how it'll be used
- Use examples: show the AI what good output looks like
- Iterate: treat it like a conversation, refine the output
- Common prompt patterns: drafting, editing, summarizing, comparing, brainstorming

**Activity:** Take one prompt and improve it through 3 iterations, comparing output quality each time.

**Takeaway:** Prompting cheat sheet with 10 template prompts for common tasks.

---

## Module 5: AI for Managers (Required for Team Leads and Above)

**Goal:** Managers understand their oversight responsibilities and can support their teams.

**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Topics:**
- Your role in AI governance (you're the first line of defense, not IT)
- How to spot shadow AI usage on your team
- When to escalate concerns vs. handle locally
- How to evaluate whether AI is actually helping your team's productivity
- Setting expectations: AI augments your team, it doesn't replace judgment
- How to have the "you're responsible for AI output" conversation

**Activity:** Role-play an incident scenario where a team member makes an AI-related mistake.

**Takeaway:** Manager checklist for monthly AI usage review with their team.

---

## Delivery Schedule

| Phase | Modules | Audience | Timeline |
|-------|---------|----------|----------|
| **Phase 1: Foundation** | Modules 1 + 2 | All staff | Before any AI tool rollout |
| **Phase 2: Tool Launch** | Module 3 | Staff using the specific tool | At tool rollout |
| **Phase 3: Optimization** | Module 4 | Power users, early adopters | 2-4 weeks after rollout |
| **Phase 4: Leadership** | Module 5 | Managers and above | Within first month |

---

## Measuring Training Effectiveness

| Metric | How to Measure | Target |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Completion rate | LMS tracking or sign-in sheets | 95% of applicable staff |
| Knowledge retention | Quiz scores on Modules 1 + 2 | 80% average |
| Tool adoption | Active users / licensed users | 70% within 60 days |
| Incident rate | AI-related incidents post-training | Decrease quarter over quarter |
| Support tickets | AI-related help desk tickets | Decrease after Module 3 |

---

*Refresh training materials quarterly as tools and policies evolve. Annual refresher for Modules 1 and 2 is recommended.*
