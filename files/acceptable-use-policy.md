## Purpose

This policy defines how employees may use artificial intelligence tools in their work. It exists to protect the organization, our clients, and our data while enabling teams to benefit from AI technology.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and vendors who use AI tools in connection with organizational work. This includes both organization-provided tools and personal AI accounts used for work purposes.

## What You Can Do

- **Draft and edit documents.** Use AI to help write emails, reports, memos, and other business documents. Always review output before sending.
- **Research and summarize.** Ask AI tools to explain concepts, summarize public information, or help with analysis.
- **Generate code and scripts.** Use AI coding assistants for development work. All AI-generated code must go through normal review processes.
- **Brainstorm and plan.** Use AI as a thinking partner for project planning, problem-solving, and ideation.
- **Translate and adapt content.** Use AI for language translation or adapting content for different audiences.

## What You Cannot Do

- **Input protected health information (PHI).** Never paste patient names, medical record numbers, diagnoses, or any data covered by HIPAA into AI tools unless the tool has a signed BAA and has been specifically approved for PHI.
- **Input student records.** Never share information protected by FERPA, including student names, grades, or educational records.
- **Share credentials or access tokens.** Never paste passwords, API keys, SSH keys, or authentication tokens into AI tools.
- **Submit confidential business data.** Financial records, trade secrets, unreleased product plans, and board materials are off-limits unless using an approved, enterprise-licensed tool with appropriate data protections.
- **Make final decisions based solely on AI output.** AI can inform decisions but cannot be the sole basis for hiring, termination, clinical, or financial decisions.
- **Represent AI output as original work** without disclosure when context requires it (e.g., published research, regulatory filings).
- **Use AI to generate content about specific individuals** without their knowledge, including performance assessments, behavioral profiles, or communications that impersonate someone.

## Data Handling Rules

Before using any data with an AI tool, check its classification:

| Data Level | Can Use with AI? | Examples |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| **Public** | ✅ Yes | Published website content, public press releases, general industry info |
| **Internal** | ⚠️ With approved tools only | Internal memos, project plans, non-sensitive operational data |
| **Confidential** | ❌ No (unless tool has specific approval) | Financial records, strategic plans, employee compensation data |
| **Restricted** | ❌ Never | PHI, student records, SSNs, credentials, legal hold materials |

*When in doubt, treat data as Confidential and ask your manager or IT.*

## Review and Accountability

- **All AI-generated content must be reviewed by a human** before it's used in any official capacity.
- **You are responsible for AI output.** If you use AI to draft something, you own the final product, including any errors.
- **Log your usage when required.** For regulated work (clinical documentation, compliance reports), note when AI assisted in creation.
- **Report problems immediately.** If an AI tool produces harmful, biased, or incorrect output that impacts operations, report it to [CONTACT] using the [Incident Response Plan](incident-response-plan.md).

---

## Consequences

Violations of this policy will be handled through the existing disciplinary process. Depending on severity, consequences may range from additional training to termination. Intentional misuse involving protected data may carry legal consequences.
