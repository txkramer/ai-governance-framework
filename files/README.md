# AI Governance Framework

A practical, ready-to-deploy governance framework for organizations adopting AI tools. Built from real-world experience implementing AI systems in healthcare and education environments.

This isn't theoretical. Every template, checklist, and guide here has been shaped by actual deployment decisions, risk conversations, and the messy reality of getting non-technical teams comfortable with AI.

---

## Who This Is For

- **IT managers and directors** rolling out AI tools across their organization
- **Team leads** who need to set guardrails without killing momentum
- **Small and mid-size organizations** that don't have a dedicated AI ethics board but still need structure
- **Anyone** tasked with writing an AI policy and staring at a blank page

## Philosophy

This framework is built on a few core beliefs:

**Governance should enable, not block.** The goal is to help your organization adopt AI faster and more safely. If your policy just says "no" to everything, people will work around it.

**Start simple, iterate often.** You don't need a 50-page policy on day one. Start with acceptable use, add risk assessment, then layer in compliance mapping as you grow.

**Write for humans.** Every document here is written in plain language. If your staff can't understand the policy, the policy doesn't work.

**Healthcare and education need extra care.** These industries handle sensitive data (patient records, student information) and face real regulatory consequences. The templates reflect that reality.

## How to Use This

1. **Start with the [Acceptable Use Policy](templates/acceptable-use-policy.md).** This is the single most important document. Customize it, get leadership sign-off, and distribute it.

2. **Evaluate your tools with the [Tool Evaluation Checklist](templates/tool-evaluation-checklist.md).** Before approving any AI tool, run it through the scoring system.

3. **Classify your data.** Use the [Data Classification Guide](risk-assessment/data-classification.md) to figure out what's off-limits for AI processing.

4. **Set up regular communication.** The [Weekly Briefing Template](briefings/weekly-briefing-template.md) keeps leadership engaged without overwhelming them.

5. **Build your team.** Use the [AI Committee Guide](guides/building-ai-committee.md) when you're ready to formalize oversight.
